Teacher Letter Recommendations

Guidelines and examples for writing personalized letters to students.

General Recommendations

  1. The first part is devoted to student's overall performance and participation in class, the second one summarizes test results and provides recommendations for future improvement.
  2. Use simpler vocabulary for younger learners (Starters, Movers, Flyers).
  3. Provide clear and specific recommendations for each child's areas for improvement.
  4. Please avoid double spaces, trailing commas, and using hyphens (-) instead of em-dashes (—).
  5. Please keep your response between 110 and 129 words.
  6. If mentioning personal details, ensure they connect to learning.
  7. Highlight strengths, but avoid praising poor effort/behavior—frame it as a kind recommendation.
  8. Send the letters in a single .docx file before creating certificates.
  9. Double-check that you submitted all the letters and didn't forget anyone.
  10. Please follow the deadlines.

Examples

Real letter samples from our teachers.

Dear Olesya,

I was extremely happy to meet you here in MMC this session! You were always on time, prepared, and ready to learn. I appreciated your attention during lessons and the way you contributed to discussions. It was great to see you enjoy learning English and improve day by day. Your test showed your progress—great job! I suggest watching TV shows or cartoons in English, writing about your day in a small notebook, and learning new words every day. That will help you keep getting better.
Hope to meet you soon!
Julia

Dear Adelina,

I was extremely happy to meet you here in MMC this session! You were an active and thoughtful student, and I really appreciated your interest in English. You always paid attention, and completed all tasks with care. It was a pleasure to teach you and watch your progress. Your test was really good—you did a great job! I recommend you continue practicing English by listening songs, watching videos with subtitles, or writing about your thoughts in a short diary. A little practice every day makes a big difference.
Hope to meet you soon!
Julia

Your future in English looks very bright.
Great job, Denis—keep it up!
